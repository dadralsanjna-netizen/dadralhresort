Nearby Attractions

Amber Fort

The majestic Amber Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring stunning architecture, mirror work, and panoramic views of Jaipur. Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh, this magnificent fort is constructed with pale yellow and pink sandstone, and white marble. The fort is known for its artistic style, blending both Hindu and Mughal elements. Don't miss the Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) with its intricate mirror work.

City Palace

The royal residence showcasing a blend of Rajasthani and Mughal architecture with beautiful courtyards and museums housing royal artifacts. The palace complex includes the Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal palaces and other buildings. The palace was built between 1729 and 1732 by Sawai Jai Singh II, the ruler of Amber. The palace complex is divided into a series of courtyards, sprawling gardens and buildings.

Hawa Mahal

The iconic "Palace of Winds" with its unique honeycomb structure featuring 953 small windows (jharokhas) built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. The five-story palace was designed to allow royal ladies to observe everyday life and festivals celebrated in the street below without being seen. The palace is constructed of red and pink sandstone, and stands at the edge of the City Palace. The structure is a fine example of Rajput architecture.

Jantar Mantar

Collection of 19 astronomical instruments built by Maharaja Jai Singh II including the world's largest stone sundial accurate to within 2 seconds. The instruments allow the observation of astronomical positions with the naked eye. The observatory is one of five built by Jai Singh II in different cities. The Samrat Yantra (Supreme Instrument) is the world's largest sundial, standing 27 meters tall. The site was added to the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2010.

Jal Mahal

The stunning "Water Palace" appearing to float in the center of Man Sagar Lake. Built in 18th century in Rajput style, with four floors submerged when the lake is full. The palace was originally constructed in 1699 and later renovated and enlarged in the 18th century by Maharaja Jai Singh II. The palace is a five-storied building, of which four floors remain underwater when the lake is full. The architecture is a blend of Mughal and Rajput styles.

Nahargarh Fort

The "Tiger Fort" offers breathtaking views of Jaipur city. Part of the defensive ring along with Amber Fort and Jaigarh Fort. Features beautiful frescoes and summer palace. Built mainly in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the fort was expanded in 1868. The fort was originally named Sudarshangarh but became known as Nahargarh after Nahar Singh Bhomia, whose spirit was said to haunt the place. The fort houses Madhavendra Bhawan, a summer palace with identical suites for the king and his queens.
